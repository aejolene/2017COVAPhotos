CATEGORY: Artifacts

    Fragments of a stoneware vessel found in fill from a pipe trench with Monticello in the background. Credit: Monticello

    2. These shell beads were uncovered from the Carter Robinson mound site in Lee County, VA. All stages of shell bead manufacture have been found at this site, concentrated in a large structure located near the mound. Credit: Maureen Meyers

    3. Overglaze pearlware ceramic sherd (left) examined using precisely angled lights to reflect the remaining transferprint residues to reveals an image of a woman wearing a high-wasted gown andspencer (right). May 5, 2014) Credit: Poplar Forest

    4. The image here is from the field school at Eyreville (44NH0507). Dozens of 17th century pipe bowls and stems were recovered. These are a few with maker marks. 5/17/2017 Credit: Mike Clem/VDHR

    5. North American Stoneware pan, recovered from Ker Place, Onancock, Virginia. May 2012. Credit: Lauren McMillan

    6. Artifact image: Robert Carter wine bottle seal recovered from the bulkhead entrance to the cellar at Coan Hall (6-15-2017). Credit: Barbara Heath

    7. A Union Army Belt Plate recovered from Washington’s Boyhood Home, where Union Army corps were entrenched in 1862, and thousands of enslaved Americans crossed the Rappahannock to Stafford from Confederate-held Fredericksburg. Inspired by Parson Weem’s biography of Washington, Lincoln visited the home site on May 23, 1862. Photo taken Feb 8 2013. Credit: The George Washington Foundation

    8. These two cathedral-style pickle bottles were found within separate trash pits at a Civil War-era encampment in the town of Falmouth. This site was excavated by Dovetail in 2016. Credit: Dovetail CRG
